Study Techniques

Here is a list of some of our favourite study techiniques.

Pomodoro Technique:

The Pomodoro technique spaces out work into short periods of time (ex. 25 mins).The technique works as follows:

1. Choose what you want to focus on and set your a timer to your desired time period.

2. Focus until the timer goes off.

3. Take a short break.

4. After 4 of these "focus periods" (AKA Pomodoros), take a longer break.

Feynman Technique:

This study technique is based on the idea that if you want to understand a concept, you try to teach it.

1. Choose a topic and write down everyting that you know about the topic.

2. Try to explain the concept in a simple manner, as if you were teaching someone who has no knowledge on the subject.

3. Go back to what you wrote and see if you were correct, check if there were places where you need to improve.

4. If there was anything that could be broken down into more simple language, go back and rewrite these parts.

Retrieval Practice:

This method focuses on the act of retrieving the information that you.

studied at a previous time. This method helps with

recalling information that can be used at a later date (ie. a test, exam, etc.)

1. Put away your notes.

2. Grab a sheet of paper and write down everything that you remember about the topic.

3. Check your notes to see if there was anything that you got wrong. Use this technique as a way to find out what you should focus on.

Managing Academic Stress:

Here are ways to manage academic stress.

Plan Ahead:

Knowing when a test is or when an assignment is due can save you a lot of trouble! Putting all of the dates of tests and when assignments are due

can give you a good overview of what needs to be done first. You can aso try to set some goals that you want to accomplish during a certain time period

such as trying all of the practice problems in a chapter before the weekend. If you set goals ask yourself these questions first.

S - Specific, What do I need to accompish?

M - Measurable, How do I know when I have accomplished my goal?

A - Achievable, Can I realistically reach my goal?

R - Relevant, Does acheiving this goal help me in any way?

T - Time-bound, How much time do I have to accomplish my goal?

Don't Fall Behind:

Falling behind in a class makes it difficult to understand what is happening in future lectures.

Classes were designed with the idea that you already understand the ideas that were taught in the previously.

You can get help by reaching out to your instructor to go over a concept that you find difficult, but it's important to understand these topics ASAP so that you can understand future topics that may buid on these.

Speak to Someone:

Some post-secondary insitutions have counselling services for their students, which would be a good place for you to talk about what you're going through and to get help.

Basic Needs:

Here are ways meeting your basic needs can improve your mental health.

Sleep:

Getting enough sleep is important becasue your body and mind need to rest. Not having

enough sleep can lead to problems with learning, focusing, and remembering things. These are

all the things that can greatly affect you as a post-secondary student.

Staying Active:

Physical acitivty can improve your self esteem, making you feel better about yourself. Being active

also causes chemical changes to your brain that improves your mood.

Being Social:

Socailising is incredibly important to maintaining good mental wellbeing, especially in a post-Covid era. Being social allows you to

make connections which gives you a sense of belonging. Being around others also allows you to recieve emotional

support as well as allows you to give it to others.
